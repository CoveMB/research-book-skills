# Search strategy guide

Use concept families, synonyms, opposing terms, methods terms, cases, and author names. Document every search. Use backward, forward, and lateral citation chaining. Mark unverified leads clearly.

For systematic review mode, first use a review-type prefilter: systematic review, scoping review, rapid review, narrative review, or critical review. Keep a PRISMA-compatible protocol snapshot only when the user needs reporting-grade documentation: review question, databases, exact strings, dates, filters, reviewer process, appraisal plan, risk of bias or quality appraisal where relevant, synthesis method, certainty or confidence limits, screening counts, dedupe count, inclusion/exclusion rules, and exclusion reasons. Treat this as optional reporting-grade structure, not the default for ordinary book research.
