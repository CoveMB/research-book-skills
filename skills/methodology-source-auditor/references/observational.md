# Observational Method Audit

## Audit questions

- What population, exposure, comparison group, outcome, and measurement process are visible?
- Are confounding, selection bias, measurement error, and missing data addressed?
- Is the design cross-sectional, cohort, case-control, panel, quasi-experimental, or descriptive?
- Does the source distinguish association from causation?

## Boundaries

Use this reference when non-randomized empirical evidence is expected to support descriptive, comparative, associative, or cautious causal claims.

## Cannot support

- Strong causal claims without design features that address confounding.
- Generalization beyond the sampled population without visible support.
- Mechanistic claims not measured by the study.
